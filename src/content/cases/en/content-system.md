## Context

Da Capo is a women's fashion retailer with a brick-and-mortar store in Rio de Janeiro and the online store I launched in December 2025. Content is a core part of the digital operation: Instagram, a blog on Nuvemshop, and email all need a steady flow of content. The team is lean, with no dedicated copywriter; content production is mine, amplified by the automations I built with Claude Code.

## The problem

Producing content channel by channel doesn't scale. Every post started from scratch: research the same theme again, rewrite the same argument for another format, hope the voice comes out consistent. And unstructured AI copy fails in two ways: it sounds generic (any brand could have written it) or it makes things up (claims about a garment that aren't true). I needed volume with brand consistency, and quality control that didn't depend on me rereading everything by hand.

## What I built

A three-layer content system, implemented as Claude Code skills and organized around the COPE principle: create once, publish everywhere.

- **Research:** a skill researches a theme and distills everything into a reusable brief: a thesis, three to five angles, the most common objection with its reframe, confirmed facts, and SEO seeds.
- **Production:** one skill per format (carousel, caption, blog post, reel script), each starting from the brief and writing as a consultant persona defined in its own file.
- **Review:** a quality layer kept separate from production, with brand review and language review.

## How it works

The flow follows a theme from research to publication:

- **Brief.** The research skill reads the available material (external references, product data, positioning) and writes the brief. I review and approve the thesis before anything moves forward. Once approved, the same brief feeds every format, with no new research per format.
- **Persona.** Each production skill writes as a consultant persona instead of following a checklist of voice rules. The persona distills the brand's voice guide: the full guide documents every voice the brand uses; the persona carries only the one that speaks in educational content. The result is copy with a consistent point of view.
- **Facts.** Production skills may only state what's in the product file or visible in the photo. Those files (commercial name, description, SEO fields, alt text) are generated by the same system, through the descriptions skill, and serve as the fact source the production skills consult.
- **Review.** Two skills kept separate from production: brand review validates the text against the positioning, the forbidden terms, and the banned structural patterns (it flags the deviation rather than rewriting); language review hunts down anglicisms, calques, and constructions translated literally from English. The writer never grades its own work.
- **Evals.** Before a skill becomes official, an iteration workspace runs the same requests with and without the skill and grades the outputs against explicit criteria: nothing invented, natural Portuguese, word count in range, hook quality. A skill only graduates when the gain shows up in the comparison.

## Results

One research session becomes four formats: the carousel, the caption, the blog post, and the reel script all come from the same brief, each in its own channel and all carrying the same argument. Voice stays consistent across channels with no manual rewriting.

The current winter campaign runs entirely on this system: eleven themes in total, seven already in production, each with its own brief, references, and derived formats.

And the evals workspace means I know what each skill improves before I trust it: the engineering habit of testing before shipping, applied to marketing content.

A generalized version of this framework, stripped of the brand, is coming soon as an open-source project.
